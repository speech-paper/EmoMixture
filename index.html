1. Abstract

Due to the lack of fine-grained emotion annotation in emotional emotional text-to-speech (ETTS) datasets, existing ETTS typically utilizes utterance-level emotion representation, neglecting the inherent complexity and variability of emotion. Moreover, the limited emotion categories in the datasets makes it difficult to capture the full diversity of human emotion. To address these, we introduce fine-grained emotion annotation, which are emotional-dimension extracted chunk-wise from the speech, to capture subtle emotional variations. Further, we propose a model-level emotion mixing method based on task vectors. Specifically, we construct task vectors through the element-wise differences between the model weights fine-tuned on specific emotion and the pretrained model weights. By combining the task vectors, a new model proficient in handling mixed emotions can be obtained. ETTS experiments validate the effectiveness of the proposed fine-grained representation and mixed-emotion method.
